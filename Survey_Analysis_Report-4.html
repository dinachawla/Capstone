🔬 Multivariate Analysis

Selected cross-tabulations where a meaningful relationship exists. Each chart shows how one dimension breaks down within another.

Test type × Tool adoption — Study.com vs. AI tools

% of respondents within each test-type group who use each tool regularly
Finding: Tool adoption varies sharply by test type. Other certification candidates use Study.com at 84% — likely because Study.com targets exactly those exams. Teaching/Praxis candidates have the lowest AI adoption (14%) and lowest Study.com usage (29%), suggesting they rely more on external platforms like Kaplan. Nursing candidates split evenly — 50% use Study.com, 50% use AI tools.

Prep stage × AI usage frequency

Count of respondents per stage, broken down by how often they use AI
Finding: AI use peaks midway through preparation — 5 of 12 midway respondents use AI daily (42%), the highest of any stage. Respondents who have completed preparation are the least AI-active (8 of 12 never use it), suggesting AI is used as an active learning aid during the journey, not for reflection or review after finishing.

Primary device × AI usage frequency

Count of respondents per device type, broken down by how often they use AI
Finding: Multi-device users are by far the most AI-active — only 1 of 15 (7%) never uses AI, compared to 12 of 25 (48%) of laptop-only users. This suggests AI fits most naturally into flexible, multi-context study workflows. Mobile-only users are split on AI adoption, but the small sample (n=6) limits conclusions.
